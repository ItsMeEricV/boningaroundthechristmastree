MERRY CHRISTMAS

'Twas the night before recording, when all through the home

Not a musician was stirring, not even a trombone;

The music was piled by the chimney with care,

In hopes that a festively plump, white-haired man, soon would be there;

His former students were nestled all snug in their beds;

While visions of trombone choir danced in their heads;

And Allison in her nose ring, and Ken in his cap,

Had just awakened Carrillo from a long winter's nap,

When out on the quad there arose such a clatter,

I sprang from my bed to see what was the matter.

When what to my wondering eyes did appear,

A small man, holding a trombone case, chewing gum with a sneer!

With a moustache, button-down shirt, and full head of white hair,

I knew in a moment, Steve Perdicaris was standing there.

"Now, Allison! Ken! Nesmith, and Carrillo! and my favorite one, Eric, I almost forgot!

Follow my tempos and breath together, you guys don’t remember anything I’ve taught!”

He spoke many more words, most too offensive to say

Before he quickly became frustrated, turned, and walked away

But I heard him exclaim, ere he drove out of view

“Hope you enjoy this holiday album, they made it for you!”
